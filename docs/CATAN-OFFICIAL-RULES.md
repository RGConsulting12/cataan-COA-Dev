# Catan — Official Rules Reference

Structured rules summary for the **base game** (*Catan*, formerly *The Settlers of Catan*). Use this document as authoritative context when recommending courses of action. Expansions are out of scope unless noted.

> **Trademark:** *Catan* is a registered trademark of Catan GmbH. This document is a gameplay reference for an advisory tool, not a reproduction of the official rulebook.

---

## 1. Objective

- First player to reach **10 victory points (VP)** on their turn wins.
- VP come from buildings, special cards, and bonuses (Longest Road, Largest Army).

---

## 2. Components (base game)

| Component | Count / notes |
|-----------|----------------|
| Terrain hexes | 19 (fields, forest, pasture, hills, mountains, desert) |
| Number tokens | 18 (2–12, no 7) — desert has no token |
| Settlements | 5 per player (1 VP each) |
| Cities | 4 per player (upgrade from settlement, 2 VP each) |
| Roads | 15 per player |
| Development cards | 25 (knights, progress, VP cards) |
| Resource cards | Lumber, brick, wool, grain, ore |
| Robber pawn | 1 — starts on desert |

**Standard 3–4 player setup:** Each player places 2 settlements + 2 roads during setup (snake draft). Each starting settlement grants resources from adjacent hexes (no robber blocking at setup).

---

## 3. Resources

| Resource | Terrain |
|----------|---------|
| Lumber | Forest |
| Brick | Hills |
| Wool | Pasture |
| Grain | Fields |
| Ore | Mountains |
| *(none)* | Desert |

When the dice total matches a hex’s number token, **each settlement** on that hex’s vertices produces **1** resource; **each city** produces **2**. The robber on a hex blocks production for that hex.

---

## 4. Turn structure

On your turn, in order:

1. **Roll dice** (2d6) — all players with buildings on matching numbers collect resources (unless robber blocks).
2. **Trade** (optional) — maritime and/or domestic.
3. **Build** (optional) — roads, settlements, cities, buy development cards.
4. **Play development cards** (optional) — see timing rules below.

You may trade and build in any order. You may play **one** development card per turn (see §8).

---

## 5. Rolling a 7

When the dice total is **7**:

1. **Discard:** Players with **more than 7** resource cards total must discard **half** (rounded down).
2. **Move robber:** Active player moves robber to a new hex (cannot stay). That hex produces nothing until robber moves again.
3. **Steal:** Active player steals **one random** resource card from a player with a settlement or city on a vertex touching the robber hex (if any). If multiple players, active player chooses victim.

No resource production occurs on a 7 roll.

---

## 6. Building costs

| Build | Cost |
|-------|------|
| Road | 1 lumber + 1 brick |
| Settlement | 1 lumber + 1 brick + 1 wool + 1 grain |
| City | 2 grain + 3 ore |
| Development card | 1 wool + 1 grain + 1 ore |

### Placement rules

**Roads**

- Must connect to your existing road network or settlement/city.
- May not pass through another player’s settlement/city.
- Maximum **15** roads per player.

**Settlements**

- Must be on an empty intersection (vertex).
- Must be **at least 2 edges** away from any other settlement or city (the “distance rule”).
- Must connect to **your** road network (except initial setup placements).
- Maximum **5** settlements + cities combined (cities replace settlements).

**Cities**

- Replace one of your settlements on the same vertex.
- Maximum **4** cities (since you start with 2 settlements from setup, total buildings cap at 5).

---

## 7. Trading

### Maritime trade (bank / harbors)

Default bank rates (no harbor):

| Give | Receive |
|------|---------|
| 4 of one resource | 1 of any resource |

**Harbors** on the board (2:1 or 3:1) improve rates for the player with a settlement/city on that coastal vertex.

### Domestic trade (players)

- Only on the **active player’s** turn.
- Active player may trade with any other player.
- **No** trading between non-active players.
- Any deal is allowed (resources for resources). **No** trading or giving away VP, cards, or future promises as binding rules objects.

---

## 8. Development cards

- Bought face-down; kept hidden until played (except VP cards).
- **Cannot** play a card bought on the **same turn** it was purchased.
- **One** development card may be played per turn (in addition to normal actions), except VP cards (see below).

### Card types (base deck composition)

| Type | Count | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|
| Knight | 14 | Move robber + steal (same as 7 robber step); counts toward Largest Army |
| Road building | 2 | Place 2 free roads |
| Year of plenty | 2 | Take any 2 resources from bank |
| Monopoly | 2 | Name a resource; all other players give you all cards of that type |
| Victory point | 5 | Worth 1 VP; revealed only when you reach 10 VP total |

### Knight / Largest Army

- Each played knight counts toward **Largest Army**.
- First player to **3** played knights takes **Largest Army** (2 VP).
- If another player exceeds your knight count, they take the title.

### Playing timing

- Knights: usually on your turn; may also be played **before** rolling dice on your turn, or **after** a 7 is rolled (before robber moves?) — standard tournament rule: knights on your turn, before or after roll, not on opponents’ turns except where house rules allow. **Base rule:** development cards are played on **your turn** only.
- Progress cards (road building, year of plenty, monopoly): your turn.
- VP cards: stay hidden; reveal when declaring 10 VP win.

---

## 9. Longest Road

- **Longest continuous road** of one color counts.
- Opponents’ settlements/cities **break** your road; roads on both sides still count separately toward your longest chain.
- Minimum **5** continuous road segments to claim.
- Worth **2 VP** while held.
- If another player builds a longer road, they take the bonus.

---

## 10. Victory point summary

| Source | VP |
|--------|-----|
| Settlement | 1 each |
| City | 2 each |
| Longest Road (≥5 segments) | 2 |
| Largest Army (≥3 knights played) | 2 |
| Development VP card | 1 each (hidden until win) |

**Win condition:** Reach **10 VP** on **your turn** (after building or playing a card that gets you to 10, or revealing hidden VP cards).

---

## 11. Robber — tactical notes

- Blocks resource production on its hex.
- Prevents building new settlements/cities on vertices touching the robber hex (cannot build on those intersections while robber is there).
- Moving robber away from a hex you need is often high value.
- Placing robber on an opponent’s high-probability hex (6 or 8) denies income.

---

## 12. Number token probabilities (2d6)

| Roll | Approx. chance |
|------|----------------|
| 2, 12 | 1/36 each |
| 3, 11 | 2/36 |
| 4, 10 | 3/36 |
| 5, 9 | 4/36 |
| 6, 8 | 5/36 |
| 7 | 6/36 (robber) |

Hexes with **6** and **8** are statistically strongest for production.

---

## 13. Common illegal moves (reject in recommendations)

- Building settlement within 1 edge of another settlement/city.
- Building road not connected to your network.
- Building without paying correct resources.
- Playing a development card bought same turn.
- Trading when it is not your turn (for domestic trades).
- Placing robber on the same hex it already occupies.
- Exceeding piece limits (5 settlements/cities total, 15 roads, 4 cities).

---

## 14. Strategy dimensions for COA analysis

When ranking courses of action, weigh:

1. **Resource engine** — probability of income vs. robber risk.
2. **Expansion** — road routes toward high-value intersections respecting distance rule.
3. **Conversion** — city upgrades on high-yield hexes (2× production).
4. **Development** — knights for army + robber control; VP cards hidden equity.
5. **Trading** — bank vs. harbor vs. player deals to unblock builds.
6. **Blocking** — deny opponent longest road or key intersections.
7. **Robber** — deny leader’s best hex; protect your own 6/8.
8. **Win threshold** — if at 8–9 VP, prioritize safe VP (city, settlement, reveal VP card, claim bonus).
